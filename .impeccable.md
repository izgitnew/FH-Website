## Design Context

### Users
Bilingual Hispanic women (primarily Spanish-speaking) in the United States, ages 30–55, seeking life coaching, holistic therapy, and personal transformation. They arrive at the site looking for emotional healing, self-love practices, and a trusted guide who understands the immigrant experience. Their mindset is aspirational but they may feel stuck in harmful patterns — the experience should make them feel seen, inspired, and ready to take action. The site is fully in Spanish.

### Brand Personality
**Warm, Bold, Empowering.** The voice is confident and uplifting — like a supportive friend who also pushes you to be better. It's never cold, clinical, or corporate. Tone should feel intimate and personal, with conviction behind every word. Rooted in the real lived experience of Juliana Henao — arriving from Colombia, starting over, and building a practice from scratch.

### Aesthetic Direction
**Warm & Organic.** Earthy, natural tones with soft gradients and rounded shapes. The palette centers on terracotta, sage green, cream, and blush — evoking warmth, growth, and richness. Typography pairs a refined serif (Playfair Display) for headlines with clean sans-serifs (Source Sans 3 for body, DM Sans for UI). Generous whitespace, soft shadows, and minimal animations (only 3 reveal elements) create a feeling of calm sophistication.

**Anti-references:** Avoid anything that feels sterile, tech-startup-y, or AI-generated. No card grids, no glassmorphism, no gradient text, no dark mode with glowing accents. Never loud or aggressive. No identical repeating containers — use border separators, flowing text, and visual hierarchy instead.

**Theme:** Light mode only. Warm white and cream backgrounds, dark charcoal text.

### Design Principles

1. **Warmth first.** Every design choice should make the visitor feel welcomed and valued. Prefer soft edges, warm colors, and human touches over sharp precision.

2. **Clarity over cleverness.** The path to action should always be obvious. CTAs stand out naturally through color and placement, not through gimmicks or urgency tactics.

3. **Breathe.** Generous whitespace and vertical rhythm create calm. Don't cram — let content rest. Sections should feel spacious and intentional.

4. **Aspiration through authenticity.** The design should feel elevated but never fake. Real testimonials in clients' own language, personal storytelling, and genuine photography matter more than polished stock imagery.

5. **Mobile-native mindset.** The audience is largely mobile. Every layout, tap target (44px minimum), and scroll interaction should feel natural on a phone first.

6. **No card syndrome.** Use border separators, flowing text, and visual hierarchy — not identical rounded-corner cards. Each section should feel hand-crafted, not templated.

### Layout Architecture
- Single-page site, 9 sections, ~5300px total height
- Fixed nav with scroll-triggered background + active section highlighting
- Card-free layout: border separators, flowing text, asymmetric grids
- Only 3 scroll-reveal animations (about photo, quote, video embed)
- Back-to-top button appears after 600px scroll
- Footer: 2-column (Brand + Conectar), no nav duplication
