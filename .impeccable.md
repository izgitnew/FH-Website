## Design Context

### Users
Ambitious women seeking life coaching, mentorship, and personal transformation. They arrive at the site looking for clarity, purpose, and a trusted guide to help them break through limiting beliefs and build a life or business they love. Their mindset is aspirational but they may feel stuck — the experience should make them feel seen, inspired, and ready to take action.

### Brand Personality
**Warm, Bold, Empowering.** The voice is confident and uplifting — like a supportive friend who also pushes you to be better. It's never cold, clinical, or corporate. Tone should feel intimate and personal, with conviction behind every word.

### Aesthetic Direction
**Warm & Organic.** Earthy, natural tones with soft gradients and rounded shapes. The palette centers on terracotta, sage green, cream, and gold — evoking warmth, growth, and richness. Typography pairs a refined serif (Playfair Display) for headlines with clean sans-serifs (Inter, DM Sans) for body and UI. Generous whitespace, soft shadows, and subtle animations create a feeling of calm sophistication. Inspired by Marie Forleo's site — aspirational yet approachable.

**Anti-references:** Avoid anything that feels sterile, tech-startup-y, or overly minimalist. No harsh blacks-on-whites, no neon accents, no sharp geometric layouts. Never loud or aggressive.

**Theme:** Light mode only. Warm white and cream backgrounds, dark charcoal text.

### Design Principles

1. **Warmth first.** Every design choice should make the visitor feel welcomed and valued. Prefer soft edges, warm colors, and human touches over sharp precision.

2. **Clarity over cleverness.** The path to action should always be obvious. CTAs stand out naturally through color and placement, not through gimmicks or urgency tactics.

3. **Breathe.** Generous whitespace and vertical rhythm create calm. Don't cram — let content rest. Sections should feel spacious and intentional.

4. **Aspiration through authenticity.** The design should feel elevated but never fake. Real testimonials, personal storytelling, and genuine photography matter more than polished stock imagery.

5. **Mobile-native mindset.** The audience is largely mobile. Every layout, tap target, and scroll interaction should feel natural on a phone first.
